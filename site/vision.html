AETHER Vision

The semantic coordination fabric for autonomous work.

AETHER is building toward a world where agents, operators, and services share one governed operational memory: replayable in time, recursive in reasoning, explicit in authority, and legible in proof.

Operational memory Derived readiness Governed authority Replayable proof

The goal is not to make agents more eloquent. The goal is to make agentic systems more trustworthy.

Why Now

Autonomous work needs a semantic center of gravity

As soon as AI participates in real operations, the hard questions stop being purely model questions. They become questions of state, authority, handoff, replay, and proof.

What is true?

The system needs a durable operational memory, not just transient context.

Who may act?

Authorization has to be derived from current facts, not patched in later.

Why?

Every important answer must survive scrutiny under pressure and after the fact.

Product Shape

Memory, intelligence, and coordination in one fabric

Memory

Replayable Truth

Exact `Current` and `AsOf` cuts let teams inspect what the system knew at any decision point.

Intelligence

Derived Understanding

Recursive reasoning follows the full dependency and authority chain instead of stopping at the first visible fact.

Coordination

Governed Action

Claims, leases, handoff, and stale-work fencing become part of the semantic model rather than application glue.

The Wedge

Start narrow. Prove trust. Then widen.

AETHER does not need to begin as a general platform. The current wedge is already strong: readiness, claimability, lease authority, handoff, fencing, replay, and proof.

Current pilot proof

  • durable SQLite-backed journal
  • deterministic `Current` and `AsOf` views
  • recursive readiness and authority reasoning
  • stale-attempt rejection and proof traces
  • authenticated HTTP pilot and operator report artifacts
