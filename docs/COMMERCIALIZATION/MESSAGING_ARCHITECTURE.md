# Messaging Architecture

## Message House

### Core message

AETHER is the semantic coordination fabric for agentic operations.

### What that means

It gives intelligent systems:

- durable operational memory
- derived understanding of readiness and authority
- governed action with replay and proof

### Why it matters

It turns AI-driven work from brittle automation into legible, governable,
auditable operations.

## One-Liners

### Executive

AETHER is the operational truth layer for autonomous work.

### Product

AETHER gives agents and operators one fabric for memory, coordination, and proof.

### Technical

AETHER combines replayable temporal state, recursive derivation, and governed coordination semantics in one kernel.

## 30-Second Version

AETHER is a semantic coordination fabric for agentic operations. It maintains a
durable record of operational facts, derives what follows from those facts,
governs who may act, and explains why the answer is true. That lets teams move
from interesting agent demos to trustworthy autonomous operations.

## 2-Minute Version

Most agent systems can generate output, but they struggle with operational
truth. State is scattered across queues, databases, logs, policies, and app
glue. AETHER brings those concerns back together. It maintains replayable state,
derives readiness and authority through recursive reasoning, fences stale work,
and preserves proof for operators and auditors. The result is not just an agent
framework. It is a semantic coordination fabric for autonomous work.

## Audience Variants

| Audience | Lead With | Avoid Leading With |
| --- | --- | --- |
| COO / Ops leader | readiness, ownership, handoff, blocked work | Datalog, SCCs, fixpoints |
| CTO / platform | governed autonomy, replay, trust, semantic control plane | branding language without proof |
| AI leader | operational memory, action control, explainability | low-level storage internals |
| Risk / compliance | replay, proof, `AsOf`, authority trail | "agent magic" framing |
| Engineer | deterministic semantics, recursive closure, provenance | vague platform slogans |

## Proof Points To Reuse

- durable SQLite-backed journal
- deterministic `Current` and `AsOf`
- recursive readiness and authority reasoning
- lease handoff and stale-attempt fencing
- authenticated HTTP pilot service
- semantic audit context
- operator-grade report artifacts
- performance baseline, drift, soak, and stress harnesses

## Claims Discipline

Say:

- "design-partner coordination pilot"
- "single-node pilot service"
- "durable replay and proof"
- "semantic coordination kernel"

Do not say:

- "production-ready general platform"
- "complete enterprise orchestration replacement"
- "full multi-agent operating system"
- "proven at planetary scale"

## Vocabulary Preferences

Prefer:

- complete dependency resolution
- governed action
- operational memory
- proof trail
- point-in-time truth
- stale-work fencing

Use technical terms only when needed:

- recursive closure
- provenance
- semi-naive evaluation
- stratified negation

## Presentation Rule

Move in this order:

1. operator question
2. business consequence
3. AETHER answer in plain language
4. proof from the pilot
5. technical explanation if the audience wants it
