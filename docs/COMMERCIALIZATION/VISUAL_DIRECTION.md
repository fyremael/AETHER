# Visual Direction

## Visual Thesis

AETHER should look like governed structure, not mascot theater and not generic
"AI" futurism.

The current visual direction based on recursive geometry is the right family.
It communicates:

- recurrence
- layered truth
- exact cuts
- propagated consequence
- traceable proof

Those ideas are far closer to AETHER's real semantics than faces, bots, or
ambient sci-fi gloss.

## Visual Grammar

Use four recurring abstractions:

- `orbit fields` for time, replay, and semantic motion
- `cell fields` for closure and dependency propagation
- `cut surfaces` for `Current` versus `AsOf`
- `trace filaments` for proof and provenance

These should stay consistent across:

- docs portal
- demos
- reports
- pitch materials
- diagrams

## Product Surfaces To Emphasize

The strongest visual surfaces are not illustrations. They are semantic views.

Prioritize:

- `Current` versus `AsOf` side-by-side comparisons
- dependency chains with resolved blockers highlighted
- authority handoff timelines across epochs
- stale-attempt fencing sequences
- proof traces from source facts to derived conclusion
- saved operator reports and semantic audit artifacts

These visuals teach the product while they persuade.

## Tone

Aim for:

- calm
- exact
- operator-grade
- architectural
- intellectually serious

Avoid:

- playful mascots
- generic robot imagery
- neon dashboard cliches
- visual noise that competes with the semantics

## Usability Emphasis

Usability should answer operator questions first.

The best user-facing views make these immediately visible:

- what is true now
- what changed
- what is blocked
- who may act
- why the answer holds

That means visual design should support:

- semantic diffs
- progressive disclosure of proof
- readable historical cuts
- clear state-to-decision flow

## Demo And Web Guidance

For public surfaces:

- lead with the phrase "semantic coordination fabric"
- show the pilot proof, not abstract promise alone
- use geometry to reinforce structure rather than decorate whitespace
- let screenshots, reports, and proof views carry credibility

For future product UI:

- make replay and comparison first-class
- treat explanation as a primary surface
- make authorization and blocked-state reasons visually explicit

## Brand Rule

If a visual cannot help a viewer understand replay, closure, authority, or
proof, it is probably not AETHER's strongest visual.
