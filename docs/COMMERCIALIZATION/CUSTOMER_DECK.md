# Customer Deck Script

This document is a more presentation-ready companion to
`docs/COMMERCIALIZATION/PITCH_DECK_OUTLINE.md`.

Use it when you need a polished external deck narrative rather than a raw slide
list.

## Slide 1: Category Claim

Title:
- `AETHER`
- `The semantic coordination fabric for agentic operations`

Speaker line:
- "We think the next critical AI infrastructure layer is not another model wrapper. It is the layer that tells autonomous systems what is true, what follows, who may act, and why."

## Slide 2: The Real Problem

Title:
- `Autonomous work breaks when truth is fragmented`

Speaker line:
- "As soon as agents participate in real operations, truth gets split across queues, logs, policies, memory stores, and app code. Coordination becomes fragile because the system has no single semantic center."

## Slide 3: The Questions Operations Actually Need Answered

Title:
- `The questions are operational, not rhetorical`

Questions:
- what is truly ready
- who is authoritative now
- what changed since the last cut of history
- why did the system authorize this action
- can we replay the decision exactly later

Speaker line:
- "If a system cannot answer these well, it cannot be trusted deeply."

## Slide 4: The AETHER Thesis

Title:
- `The center of gravity should be a semantic kernel`

Speaker line:
- "AETHER combines append-only history, deterministic resolution, recursive derivation, and explanation in one kernel. That lets the system maintain operational truth rather than approximate it after the fact."

## Slide 5: What AETHER Combines

Title:
- `Memory + intelligence + governed action`

Columns:
- Memory: replayable `Current` and `AsOf`
- Intelligence: derived readiness, authority, and consequence
- Coordination: claims, leases, handoff, fencing
- Proof: source facts and explanation traces

Speaker line:
- "This is why we call it a semantic coordination fabric. The pieces matter most when they live together."

## Slide 6: Why This Matters For Agents

Title:
- `LLMs provide fluency. AETHER provides operational truth.`

Speaker line:
- "Models can suggest and synthesize. AETHER gives them a governed operating environment: durable memory, explicit authority, replayable decisions, and proof."

## Slide 7: The Pilot Proof

Title:
- `The first coordination slice already runs`

Show:
- readiness across dependency chains
- lease handoff
- stale-attempt rejection
- durable replay
- authenticated HTTP surface
- operator report artifacts

Speaker line:
- "We are not asking you to believe a diagram. We can already run a narrow but credible coordination story end to end."

## Slide 8: What The Operator Sees

Title:
- `What is true, what changed, and why`

Show:
- current authorization
- `AsOf` authorization
- fenced stale attempt
- proof trace
- saved report artifact

Speaker line:
- "The operator surface is the real test. If the system cannot explain itself under pressure, it is not ready to govern work."

## Slide 9: Entry Wedge

Title:
- `Start where coordination errors are expensive`

Best wedge examples:
- service operations
- incident response
- compliance-heavy internal workflows
- human-plus-agent exception handling

Speaker line:
- "We do not need to start with every workflow. We need to start where readiness, ownership, and authority are materially valuable."

Support note:
- use `docs/COMMERCIALIZATION/CANONICAL_USE_CASES.md` as the follow-on ladder if the audience wants to see how the wedge expands from pilot proof to platform horizon

## Slide 10: What This Becomes

Title:
- `From pilot kernel to control plane`

Progression:
- design-partner pilot
- durable managed service
- operator dashboards and semantic diffs
- builder surfaces and connectors
- intelligence plane for governed agents

Speaker line:
- "The pilot is narrow by design. The destination is much larger: the semantic control plane for autonomous work."

## Slide 11: Why It Can Be Big

Title:
- `This is infrastructure, not ornament`

Speaker line:
- "If agentic systems become real participants in operations, something like AETHER will be necessary. The opportunity is large because it sits at the convergence of memory, reasoning, policy, coordination, and explanation."

## Slide 12: The Ask

Title:
- `Work with us on a narrow, high-conviction pilot`

Calls to action:
- identify a coordination workflow that is expensive to get wrong
- model the readiness and authority semantics
- prove replay, explanation, and fencing
- evaluate whether AETHER should become a deeper control layer

## Deck Guidance

- Lead with operator pain, not low-level semantics.
- Separate current proof from platform vision clearly.
- Use the flagship demo and pilot report as evidence anchors.
- Save terms like `recursive closure` for the appendix or technical Q&A.
